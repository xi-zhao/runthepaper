# Derivation

Let $S=\sqrt{\Delta}-A>0$. Then

$$
\omega_-^2=k^2(k^2-2S).
$$

Inside the unstable band, $\omega_-=i\gamma$ with

$$
\gamma=k\sqrt{2S-k^2},\qquad
\frac{d\gamma}{dk}=\frac{2(S-k^2)}{\sqrt{2S-k^2}}.
$$

It is positive only for `k^2<S`, and differentiating with respect to `y=k^2` gives a strictly negative derivative on `0<y<S`. Its values approach zero as `y -> S` but never include zero. Therefore the frozen “minimum positive” does not exist.

On the source's stable branch,

$$
\omega_-=k\sqrt{k^2-2S},\qquad
\frac{d\omega_-}{dk}=\frac{2(k^2-S)}{\sqrt{k^2-2S}}.
$$

This diverges at $k_{\max}=\sqrt{2S}$ and for large $k$, and its unique stationary point is

$$
k_{\min}=\sqrt{3S},\qquad V_{\rm 2c}=4\sqrt{S}.
$$
