# Derivation

For fixed masses, outer semimajor axis, and inner eccentricity,

$$
\gamma(a_{\rm in})=\frac{4Gm_{12}^3}{c^2m_1m_2a_{\rm in}}
\left(\frac{a_{\rm out}}{a_{\rm in}}\right)^{7/2}
\frac{1}{1-e_{\rm in}^2}\propto a_{\rm in}^{-9/2}.
$$

Thus the resonance location follows without iterative fitting:

$$
a_{\rm in,res}=a_{\rm in,0}\gamma_0^{2/9}.
$$

Expanding the source Hamiltonian through quadratic order in outer eccentricity and multiplying by the harmless factor six gives

$$
\hat{\mathcal H}_{\rm lin}=-D e_{\rm out}^2+C e_{\rm out}\cos\Delta\varpi,
\qquad D=-\frac32B-3\gamma.
$$

On the zero-energy separatrix at aligned periapses,

$$
e_{\rm out}(-D e_{\rm out}+C)=0,
\qquad e_{\rm out,peak}=\frac{C}{D}.
$$

For a general reduced energy, direct solution of the displayed Hamiltonian is

$$
e_{\rm out}=\frac12\left[\beta\cos\phi\pm
\sqrt{\beta^2\cos^2\phi-4\hat{\mathcal H}/D}\right].
$$

The PRL supplement prints a plus sign before `4 H/D`; its plotted curves therefore correspond to the negative of the stated energy sign.
