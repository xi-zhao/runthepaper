# Lessons learned

## New Failure Modes

- A benchmark can combine a direct out-of-window PRL formula source with an in-window non-PRL narrative source.
- Closely related papers may define a dimensionless rate ratio as reciprocals; both cross unity but move in opposite directions.
- A paper's plotted quadratic roots can expose an energy-sign label error that leaves its separatrix unchanged.

## Reusable Checks Or Tools

- Source-lineage graph: separate formula source, narrative source, venue, and date.
- Convention fingerprint: compare numerator, denominator, and monotonic direction for every dimensionless ratio.
- Substitute plotted roots back into the Hamiltonian instead of trusting color labels.
