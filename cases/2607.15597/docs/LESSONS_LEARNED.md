# Lessons learned

## What generalized well

1. A single branch-conditioned forced-oscillator model generated phase-space
   paths, populations, concurrence, operating-point tables, and circular-state
   approximations without scattering rules across plotting code.
2. The ion-chain Hessian and analytic piecewise integrals made simultaneous
   mode closure both fast and directly testable.
3. CSV-first output kept every visible curve traceable to generated numbers.
4. Formula direction must be checked before visual imitation. In Fig. 4(b),
   the stated `2 pT / Nops` law decreases while the raster visually increases;
   scientific and presentation lanes must therefore remain separate.
5. Exact canvas dimensions and high layout overlap do not imply pixel-exact
   reproduction. Fonts, unpublished curve points, and editor post-processing
   remain genuine evidence boundaries.

## Remaining boundary

- The MQDT/Stark map lacks its basis, energy window, and state-tracking data.
- Exact qLDPC Monte Carlo requires code matrices, circuits, decoder settings,
  random seeds, and stopping criteria.
- Thermal and circular curves require the full open-system notebook to move
  beyond the published analytic feature contract.

The durable rule is to label exact, reconstructed, source-only, and proxy
targets independently. Visual polish may explain a result, but it cannot
upgrade incomplete scientific provenance.
